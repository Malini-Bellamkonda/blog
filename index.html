A little while ago, the indian Forced launched ‘OPERATION SINDOOR’, hitting terrorist infrastructure in Pakistan and Pakistan-occupied Jammu and Kashmir from where terrorist attacks against India have been planned and directed. Altogether, nine (9) sites have been targeted. Our actions have been focused, measured and non-escalatory in nature. No Pakistani military facilities have been targeted. India has demonstrated considerable restraint in selection of targets and method of execution. These steps come in the wake of the barbaric Pahalgam terrorist attack in which 25 Indians and one Nepali citizen were murdered. We are living up to the commitment that those responsible for this attack will be held accountable.

Why Operation Sindoor is important?

Operation Sindhoor proved that India is always ready to protect its people, no matter where they are. It also improved India's reputation for rapid humanitarian response at a global level.

1. Narendra Modi (Strategic Leadership & National Command Oversight)

check

As the supreme executive authority of India, Prime Minister Narendra Modi oversaw the high-level coordination and approval of Operation Sindoor. His leadership ensured swift inter-agency collaboration between the armed forces, intelligence units, and central/state governments. He was briefed at every stage and maintained direct communication with key military officials to monitor the evolving situation. Modi emphasized a balance between assertive national defense and civilian safety, which shaped the operation's strategic intent. His public statement post-operation reinforced India's zero-tolerance stance on terrorism. Known for decisive leadership, he ensured that national security efforts remained sharp, swift, and unified. His presence was not just symbolic but actively operational through real-time oversight.

2. Wing Commander Vyomika Singh (Air Support & Surveillance)

check

Wing Commander Vyomika Singh served a crucial role in Operation Sindoor by coordinating air surveillance and helicopter support missions. With over 2,500 flight hours, she expertly piloted high-altitude helicopters in difficult terrains. She became one of the two women officers to represent the armed forces during the official press briefing, marking a historic moment. Vyomika ensured precision air-drop support and aerial evacuation for injured troops. Her calm leadership under pressure became a symbol of confidence and professionalism. A permanent commissioned officer, she broke gender barriers in strategic air roles. Her efforts greatly enhanced real-time intel gathering through aerial reconnaissance.

3. Colonel Sofia Qureshi (Logistics & Field Coordination – Key Female Officer in Operation Sindoor)

check

Colonel Sofia Qureshi is a trailblazing officer in the Indian Army. She played a crucial role in Operation Sindoor by managing logistics and field-level coordination. Her duties included troop deployment, supply chain management, and resource mobilization. She ensured smooth operations even in high-pressure combat situations. (First Indian woman to lead an Indian Army contingent in a foreign military exercise – UN Peacekeeping, 2016) Her leadership inspired many, especially young women in uniform. She represents strength, discipline, and smart strategic thinking in modern warfare.

4. Lieutenant General Rajiv Ghai (Ground Operations Strategy)

check

Lt. General Rajiv Ghai was the head of ground strategy in Operation Sindoor and served as Director General of Military Operations. A seasoned officer of the Kumaon Regiment, he managed troop movements and strike planning across complex terrains. His command ensured minimal casualties during high-risk operations, especially near border zones. Known for a calm and calculated approach, he balanced aggressive tactics with humanitarian concern. He also briefed the nation on military preparedness and coordination across forces. Ghai has previously commanded counter-insurgency missions and continues to serve as Deputy Chief of Army Staff. His guidance was pivotal in uniting forces under a single, adaptive strategy.

5. Air Marshal A.K. Bharti (Precision Strike Coordination)

check

Air Marshal Awadhesh Kumar Bharti supervised air operations during Operation Sindoor, ensuring successful precision strikes on militant targets. A decorated pilot and strategist, he oversaw the integration of unmanned aerial vehicles and air-to-ground missile targeting. Under his leadership, IAF aircraft executed operations with pinpoint accuracy and ensured no civilian casualties. He also reinforced India’s drone defense posture using indigenous systems like Akash. Known for his disciplined training legacy, he commands immense respect in Air Force circles. During the mission, his leadership maintained air dominance and operational readiness. He continues to serve as Deputy Chief of Air Staff, shaping future air strategies.
